What are Computer Graphics?

Computer graphics are a research field at the intersection of computer science and design that involves generating, manipulating, and rendering visual content using computational methods. It encompasses techniques for creating both 2D and 3D imagery, including raster graphics (pixel-based images), vector graphics (mathematical shape representations), and computer-generated imagery (CGI).

Main Advantage of Computer Graphics

The primary advantage is versatility and efficiency. Computer graphics enables rapid prototyping, iteration, and visualization without physical materials. It revolutionizes industries by allowing designers to create complex simulations, realistic animations, and interactive experiences that would be impossible or prohibitively expensive to produce physically. Medical imaging, engineering design, entertainment, and virtual reality all benefit from the ability to visualize and manipulate digital representations in real-time.

Main Disadvantage of Cryptography

The main disadvantage is the steep learning curve and resource intensity. Mastering computer graphics requires substantial technical knowledge spanning mathematics (geometry, linear algebra), programming, and specialized software tools. Additionally, creating high-quality graphics demands significant computational power and time, particularly for rendering complex 3D scenes or simulations. This creates barriers to entry and can make production costs substantial for professional-grade work.
